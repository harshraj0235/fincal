Your CIBIL Score is a 3-digit number ranging from 300 to 900, which acts as an indicator of your creditworthiness. Most lenders check your CIBIL Score before approving a loan or credit card application. A higher score (generally above 750) increases your chances of getting loans approved quickly and at favorable interest rates.

Factors Affecting Your CIBIL Score:

  • Payment History (30%): Timely payments of EMIs and credit card bills are crucial. Defaults or delays significantly hurt your score.
  • Credit Utilization (25%): The amount of credit you use relative to your total available credit. Keep it below 30%.
  • Length of Credit History (20%): Older, well-managed accounts show stability.
  • Credit Mix (10%): A healthy mix of secured (home, car loans) and unsecured (personal loans, credit cards) credit is positive.
  • New Credit (10%): Too many new credit applications in a short period can be seen as risky.
  • Other Factors (5%): Includes public records, inquiries, etc.

Free DIY Guide to Improve Your CIBIL Score:

  1. Pay EMIs/Bills on Time: Set reminders and automate payments. This is the single most important factor.
  2. Keep Credit Utilization Low: Try to use less than 30% of your total credit limit.
  3. Avoid Multiple Loan Applications: Each application leads to a 'hard inquiry' which can temporarily drop your score.
  4. Maintain a Healthy Credit Mix: Show you can manage different types of credit responsibly.
  5. Review Your Credit Report Regularly: Check for errors and get them rectified immediately. You can get one free credit report annually from CIBIL.
  6. Don't Close Old Accounts: Longer credit history is beneficial.

How to Get Your CIBIL Report

You can get one free credit report per year from each credit bureau. Here's how to get your CIBIL report:

  1. Visit the official CIBIL website (www.cibil.com)
  2. Register with your personal details
  3. Verify your identity with PAN or other ID proof
  4. Access your free credit report
