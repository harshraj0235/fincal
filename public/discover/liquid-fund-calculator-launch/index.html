Why Keeping Money in a Savings Account is Making You Poorer in 2026

By MoneyCal Team • 4 जुलाई 2026

For decades, the traditional Indian savings bank account has been the default place to park emergency funds and idle cash. However, with retail inflation hovering around 5-6% and most major banks offering a paltry 2.7% to 3.5% interest on savings accounts, your money is actually losing purchasing power every single day.

The "Negative Real Return" Trap

Let's do the math. If your savings account gives you 3% interest but inflation is at 5%, your real return is -2%. This means the ₹1 Lakh you saved today will effectively buy 2% less goods next year. Over a 5-year period, the compounding effect of this negative return can severely erode your hard-earned wealth.

Advertisement

The Smart Alternative: Liquid Mutual Funds

A Liquid Fund is a category of debt mutual fund that invests in highly secure, short-term money market instruments (like 91-day Treasury Bills and Commercial Papers). Here is why smart investors are shifting their idle cash to liquid funds:

  • Better Yields: Liquid funds historically aim to deliver returns in the range of 6.5% to 7.5% per annum, which is nearly double what traditional savings accounts offer.
  • High Liquidity: You can redeem your money anytime. Most funds process withdrawals within 24 hours, and some even offer instant redemption (up to ₹50,000 or 90% of the value) via IMPS within 30 minutes!
  • Low Risk: Because they invest in instruments maturing in just 91 days, they are practically immune to the interest rate fluctuations that affect long-term debt funds.

Taxation (The Fine Print)

As per the latest tax rules effective April 1, 2023, gains from liquid funds are added to your total income and taxed at your applicable income tax slab rate. Even without the historical indexation benefits, the pre-tax differential (e.g., 7% vs 3%) still leaves you with a higher post-tax maturity amount than a standard savings account.

Conclusion: Your emergency fund should be easily accessible, but that doesn't mean it shouldn't grow. Make your money work as hard as you do.
