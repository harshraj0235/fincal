Leaked: The Unbelievable Starting Price and Features of the Upcoming Tata Sierra EV

By Auto Tech Review • 4 जुलाई 2026

Nostalgia is a powerful selling point, but when it is combined with cutting-edge electric vehicle technology, it creates an absolute market disruptor. The automotive world was sent into a frenzy when Tata Motors officially launched the highly anticipated Tata Sierra EV on June 30, 2026. Reviving an iconic 90s nameplate, the new Sierra EV masterfully blends retro design cues with state-of-the-art electric architecture. But it is the shockingly aggressive pricing that has everyone talking.

Aggressive Pricing for a Premium SUV

Before the launch, industry analysts predicted a premium price tag north of Rs 25 lakh for the base model. Tata Motors, however, had other plans. The Sierra EV launched with a highly disruptive starting ex-showroom price of just Rs 18.79 lakh for the base 'Pure' variant.

Advertisement

The pricing strategy clearly targets the heart of the mid-size SUV market, challenging both internal combustion engine heavyweights and emerging EV rivals. For those seeking maximum performance, the top-tier 'Empowered A' variant, featuring a dual-motor All-Wheel Drive (AWD) setup, tops out at Rs 25.99 lakh. With bookings already open and deliveries scheduled to begin on July 15, 2026, dealerships are reportedly overwhelmed with inquiries.

Under the Hood: Range and Performance

Built on Tata’s dedicated Acti.ev+ architecture, the Sierra EV offers two substantial battery pack options designed to eliminate range anxiety:

Advertisement
    • The 63 kWh Battery: Available on lower variants, it delivers an impressive MIDC range of up to 565 km. • The 75 kWh Battery: Reserved for the top trims, this massive pack pushes the range up to an astounding 665 km on a single charge.
Performance enthusiasts will be drawn to the dual-motor AWD version, which features a dedicated "Boost Mode" capable of launching the heavy SUV from 0-100 km/h in a blistering 5.8 seconds.

Charging is equally impressive. The vehicle supports DC fast charging up to 120 kW, allowing the battery to juice up from 20% to 80% in approximately 26 minutes.

Retro-Modern Design Meets Tech Luxury

Aesthetically, the Sierra EV is a triumph. It successfully translates the rugged, boxy stance of the original into a sleek, contemporary five-door format. It lovingly retains the iconic wraparound rear glass that defined the original Sierra, while adding modern touches like a flush full-width LED light bar.

Advertisement

Inside, the cabin is a tech paradise. The dashboard is dominated by a modern triple-screen layout. Passengers are treated to a massive panoramic sunroof, a 12-speaker JBL sound system with Dolby Atmos, and ventilated seats. A unique "Boss mode" allows rear passengers to push the front passenger seat forward for maximum legroom.

The Verdict

With Level 2 ADAS, V2L (Vehicle-to-Load) capabilities, and a 540-degree camera system included, the Tata Sierra EV is not just a trip down memory lane; it is a serious technological powerhouse. By pricing it so aggressively, Tata has ensured that the Sierra nameplate will once again dominate Indian roads.
