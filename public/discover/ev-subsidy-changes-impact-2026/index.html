EV Subsidy Cuts 2026: How the New Policy Impacts Electric Car and Bike Prices

By MoneyCal Team • 4 जुलाई 2026

The End of the FAME II Era

The Indian electric vehicle (EV) market has enjoyed a massive growth spurt, heavily fueled by the generous subsidies offered under the FAME II (Faster Adoption and Manufacturing of Electric Vehicles) scheme. However, as the industry matures, the government is gradually rolling back these financial incentives to foster a self-sustaining ecosystem.

The latest policy changes have significantly reduced the direct subsidy amount available to consumers purchasing new electric two-wheelers and four-wheelers. This move has sparked concerns among manufacturers about a potential short-term dip in sales volume as prices inevitably rise.

Advertisement

What Are the New Subsidy Rules?

  • Two-Wheeler Cap Reduced: The maximum subsidy for electric two-wheelers has been slashed by nearly 30%, significantly increasing the upfront cost for popular models like Ola, Ather, and TVS iQube.
  • Four-Wheeler Phased Out: Direct consumer subsidies for electric cars are being aggressively phased out, with the focus shifting entirely towards supporting public transport and commercial EV fleets.
  • PLI Scheme Focus: The government is shifting its financial support from consumer subsidies to the Production-Linked Incentive (PLI) scheme, rewarding manufacturers for localizing battery production and component manufacturing.
  • State-Level Variations: While central subsidies are shrinking, several state governments continue to offer localized incentives, making EV prices highly variable depending on where you register the vehicle.

Will EV Sales Slow Down?

Industry experts predict a temporary speed bump in EV adoption rates as consumers adjust to the higher price tags. The immediate reaction to the subsidy cut is likely to be a delay in purchase decisions by price-sensitive buyers.

However, the long-term outlook remains highly bullish. The Total Cost of Ownership (TCO) for EVs is still significantly lower than petrol/diesel vehicles due to massive savings on fuel and maintenance. As battery technology improves and raw material costs stabilize, the upfront cost of EVs is expected to naturally align with traditional internal combustion engine (ICE) vehicles over the next few years.

Buyer Advice: If you are planning to buy an EV, factor in the Total Cost of Ownership over 5 years rather than just the upfront sticker price. The fuel savings usually offset the higher initial cost.

The Manufacturer Response

To counter the price shock, EV manufacturers are rapidly innovating. We are seeing the introduction of lower-spec, more affordable variants of popular models. Additionally, companies are heavily investing in localization to benefit from the PLI scheme, which will eventually allow them to reduce prices organically.
