SEBI's New F&O Rules: How They Impact Retail Traders and Market Volumes

By MoneyCal Team • 4 जुलाई 2026

The Boom and the Backlash in F&O Trading

The Indian stock market has witnessed an unprecedented explosion in retail participation in the Futures & Options (F&O) segment over the last few years. Driven by the gamification of trading apps, zero-brokerage models, and the allure of quick profits, millions of new investors have flocked to derivatives trading.

However, this surge has alarmed the Securities and Exchange Board of India (SEBI). Studies conducted by the regulator consistently show that over 90% of retail traders lose money in the F&O segment. In response, SEBI has proposed and implemented a series of stringent regulations designed to curb speculative trading and protect inexperienced investors.

Key Regulatory Changes

  • Increased Lot Sizes: SEBI is mandating a significant increase in the minimum contract lot size for index options. This raises the capital requirement to enter a trade, acting as a barrier for small retail traders.
  • Upfront Margin Collection: Stricter norms for the collection of upfront margins are being enforced, ensuring that brokers do not provide excessive leverage to clients.
  • Fewer Expiries: The regulator is looking to rationalise the number of weekly expiry contracts. The current trend of daily expiries across various indices encourages lottery-style trading behavior, which SEBI wants to curb.
  • Enhanced Risk Disclosures: Brokers must now prominently display risk warnings and client loss statistics on their platforms before users can execute F&O trades.

Impact on Brokers and Market Liquidity

These regulatory headwinds have caused jitters among discount brokers, whose revenue models are heavily reliant on high-frequency F&O trading volumes. A reduction in retail participation will likely impact their brokerage income and potentially lead to consolidation in the broking industry.

Furthermore, there are concerns about market liquidity. Retail traders, despite their losses, provide significant liquidity to the derivatives market. A sudden drop in retail volumes could lead to wider bid-ask spreads and increased volatility for institutional investors.

Investor Advice: While F&O trading can offer high returns, it is a complex, high-risk instrument. Retail investors should focus on long-term wealth creation through cash equities and mutual funds rather than short-term speculation.

A Shift Towards Cash Markets?

If SEBI's measures successfully cool down the F&O frenzy, we might witness a healthy migration of retail capital back to the cash equity segment. This could lead to a more stable and fundamentally driven market, shifting focus away from speculative expiry day moves.

  • To raise the capital requirement and deter small, inexperienced retail traders
  • To encourage more foreign institutional investment
  • To align with international market standards
