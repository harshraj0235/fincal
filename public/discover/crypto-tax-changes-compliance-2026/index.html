Crypto Taxes in 2026: What Investors Need to Know About the New Compliance Rules

By MoneyCal Team • 4 जुलाई 2026

The Taxman is Watching Your Crypto Trades

With Bitcoin and other major cryptocurrencies surging in value, Indian investors are once again flocking to digital assets. However, the regulatory environment is far less forgiving than in previous cycles. The Income Tax Department is leveraging advanced data analytics to track crypto transactions and crack down on tax evasion.

The government has made it clear that while trading crypto is not illegal, evading taxes on crypto gains will attract severe penalties. Many investors have recently received notices from the IT department for failing to declare their Virtual Digital Asset (VDA) transactions in previous assessment years.

Advertisement

The 30% Rule: A Quick Refresher

  • Flat 30% Tax: Any income from the transfer of VDAs (cryptocurrencies, NFTs) is taxed at a flat rate of 30%, irrespective of your income tax slab.
  • No Deductions Allowed: No deduction in respect of any expenditure (other than the cost of acquisition) or allowance is allowed while computing such income.
  • No Set-Off of Losses: Losses incurred from the transfer of one crypto asset cannot be set off against gains from another crypto asset or any other income source.
  • 1% TDS: A 1% Tax Deducted at Source (TDS) is applicable on all crypto transactions exceeding specific thresholds, ensuring a transaction trail for the government.

The Offshore Exchange Crackdown

To evade the 1% TDS, many Indian traders migrated to offshore, non-compliant crypto exchanges. The government has aggressively countered this by blocking the URLs of several prominent offshore platforms and issuing show-cause notices.

Investors using these platforms face significant risks. Transferring assets back to compliant Indian exchanges to cash out to INR now triggers intense scrutiny and requires detailed documentation of the origin of funds.

Compliance Tip: Always use Financial Intelligence Unit (FIU) registered crypto exchanges in India. Ensure all transactions are accurately recorded in your Annual Information Statement (AIS) and filed under Schedule VDA in your ITR.

How to Prepare for Tax Season

Calculating crypto taxes can be complex due to the volume of trades and the nature of crypto-to-crypto transactions. It is highly recommended that investors use specialized crypto tax software that can integrate with exchange APIs to automatically calculate gains, losses, and TDS liabilities.
