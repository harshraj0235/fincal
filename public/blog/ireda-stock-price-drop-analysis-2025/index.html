Barely a Year Ago vs. Today—How IREDA Went From ₹310 to ₹160 Fast!

By MoneyCal Team · 11 जुलाई 2025 · 10 मिनट पढ़ें

Introduction

Less than a year ago, in July 2024, shares of the Indian Renewable Energy Development Agency (IREDA) soared to an all-time high of ₹310, fueled by optimism in the renewable energy sector and strong financial performance. By July 2025, the stock had plummeted to around ₹160, a nearly 49% decline, with a sharp 6% drop to ₹159 following the Q1 FY26 results announced on July 10, 2025. This article explores the reasons behind this dramatic fall, the impact of the latest earnings, and whether this presents an opportunity or a risk for investors.

About IREDA

विज्ञापन

Established in 1987, IREDA is a government-owned non-banking financial company under the Ministry of New and Renewable Energy. As a 'Navratna' PSU with a 71.76% government stake, it plays a pivotal role in financing renewable energy projects, including solar, wind, and hydro. With a loan book of ₹79,941 crore as of June 2025, IREDA is central to India’s goal of achieving 500 GW of renewable energy capacity by 2030.

The Rise to ₹310

In July 2024, IREDA’s stock hit ₹310, driven by robust financials and market enthusiasm for renewable energy. The company reported a 30% year-on-year increase in net profit to ₹384 crore in Q1 FY25, with loan sanctions soaring 386% to ₹9,210 crore. This performance, coupled with IREDA’s 'Navratna' status and government backing, fueled investor optimism, pushing the stock to a price-to-book ratio of 12.5, significantly higher than peers like Power Finance Corporation.

Factors Behind the Decline

विज्ञापन

Despite the initial surge, several factors contributed to the stock’s decline from ₹310 to around ₹170 by early July 2025, before the Q1 FY26 results:

  • High Valuations: IREDA’s price-to-book ratio of 12.5 in July 2024 was deemed unsustainable compared to peers, triggering a market correction.
  • RBI Rejection: The RBI denied IREDA’s request for a ₹170 crore equity investment in a 900 MW hydro project in Nepal, dampening expansion hopes.
  • Deteriorating Asset Quality: By Q3 FY25, gross NPAs rose to 2.68% from 2.19%, and net NPAs increased to 1.5%, signaling loan portfolio risks.
  • Exposure to Gensol Engineering: A ₹470 crore exposure to Gensol Engineering, including ₹254.9 crore in term loans, became problematic as repayment issues emerged.
  • Tariff Pressure: Global tariffs on renewable components and monetary policy shifts impacted IREDA’s net interest margin, reported at 3.33% in Q3 FY25.

Q1 FY26 Results: The Tipping Point

The Q1 FY26 results, announced on July 10, 2025, intensified the decline. IREDA reported a 36% year-on-year drop in net profit to ₹247 crore from ₹384 crore, despite a 29% revenue increase to ₹1,947 crore. The profit decline was driven by a 60% surge in expenses to ₹1,655 crore and a 180% quarter-on-quarter increase in provisions to ₹363 crore. Gross NPAs spiked to 4.13% from 2.45%, with ₹730 crore in loans to Gensol Engineering classified as NPAs, prompting insolvency proceedings.

Market Reaction and Analyst Views

Following the Q1 FY26 results, IREDA’s stock fell 6.25% to ₹159.05 on July 11, 2025, reducing its market capitalization to ₹45,678 crore. The stock is down 48.7% from its ₹310 peak. Technical analysts note a support zone at ₹160–165, with a potential drop to ₹155 if breached, and resistance at ₹175. While some analysts maintain a 'Buy' rating due to IREDA’s long-term potential, others, like Vaibhav Vidwani from Bonanza, highlight concentration risks, with 59% of the loan book tied to the top 20 exposures.

Investment Outlook

The sharp decline may tempt investors to buy the dip, but risks remain. The high concentration in IREDA’s loan portfolio and rising NPAs suggest challenges in risk management. However, its robust operational growth, government backing, and a planned ₹5,000 crore QIP at ₹173 per share signal potential for recovery. Investors should monitor NPA resolution, particularly with Gensol Engineering, and Q2 FY26 earnings for signs of improvement. Consulting a financial advisor is recommended.

What’s Next for IREDA?

  • NPA Resolution: Progress on recovering ₹730 crore from Gensol Engineering through insolvency proceedings.
  • Cost Management: Addressing the 60% expense surge to improve profitability.
  • Capital Raising: Effective deployment of the ₹5,000 crore QIP to strengthen the balance sheet.
  • Sector Growth: Leveraging India’s renewable energy targets to drive loan book expansion.

Conclusion

IREDA’s stock price journey from ₹310 to ₹160 reflects a mix of overvaluation, regulatory hurdles, and asset quality concerns, exacerbated by a disappointing Q1 FY26. While the renewable energy sector’s long-term prospects and IREDA’s government support offer hope, investors must weigh the risks of NPAs and margin pressures. Careful monitoring of future developments will be key to determining if IREDA can rebound.
