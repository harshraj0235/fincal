IREDA Shares Plunge 6% AFTER Shocking Q1 Results—Is This the Start of a Crash?

By MoneyCal Team · 11 जुलाई 2025 · 10 मिनट पढ़ें

1. Introduction

The Indian Renewable Energy Development Agency (IREDA) saw its shares drop by approximately 6% to ₹159 on July 11, 2025, following its Q1 FY26 results announced on July 10, 2025. The results revealed a 36% year-on-year decline in net profit to ₹247 crore, despite a 29% increase in revenue to ₹1,947 crore. This mixed performance, coupled with a rise in non-performing assets (NPAs), has sparked concerns about whether this is a temporary setback or the start of a crash. This article analyzes the financials, market reactions, and what lies ahead for IREDA.

2. Q1 FY26 Financial Performance

विज्ञापन

IREDA’s Q1 FY26 results showed strong operational growth but significant profitability challenges. Net profit fell 36% year-on-year to ₹247 crore from ₹384 crore, driven by a 60% surge in expenses to ₹1,655 crore. Revenue from operations grew 29% to ₹1,947 crore, and net interest income rose 36% to ₹691 crore. The loan book expanded 27% to ₹79,941 crore, with loan sanctions up 29% to ₹11,740 crore and disbursements up 31% to ₹6,981 crore. However, gross NPAs rose to 4.13% from 2.45%, and net NPAs increased to 2.05% from 1.35%.

3. Reasons for the Profit Decline

  • Gensol Engineering Exposure: Loans worth ₹730 crore to Gensol Engineering and its subsidiary were classified as NPAs, including ₹510 crore in term loans and ₹219 crore in working capital facilities. IREDA has initiated insolvency proceedings.
  • Other Borrower Issues: An additional ₹783 crore exposure shifted to NPA status, pushing Stage 3 assets up 77% to ₹3,302 crore.
  • High Expense Growth: Expenses surged 60% year-on-year to ₹1,655 crore, with provisions increasing 180% quarter-on-quarter to ₹363 crore.

4. Management Response and Strategic Outlook

विज्ञापन

Despite challenges, IREDA’s management remains optimistic. Chairman Pradip Kumar Das emphasized the company’s role in India’s renewable energy sector, supported by a 27% loan book growth and ‘Navratna’ PSU status. IREDA is addressing NPAs through legal actions and has approval for 5-year Capital Gain Tax Exemption Bonds to raise low-cost capital. A ₹5,000 crore QIP at ₹173 per share was approved in June 2025 to strengthen the capital base.

5. Market Reaction and Analyst Perspectives

The market saw IREDA’s shares fall 6.25% to ₹159.05, reducing its market cap to ₹45,678 crore. The stock is down 48.7% from its ₹310 peak in July 2024. Technical analysts note a support zone at ₹160–165 and resistance at ₹175. Analysts like Vaibhav Vidwani highlight concentration risks, with 59% of the loan book tied to the top 20 exposures, but two of three analysts maintain a ‘Buy’ rating due to IREDA’s growth prospects.

6. Is This the Start of a Crash?

The 6% drop is likely a correction, not a crash. Strong fundamentals, including 29% revenue growth and government backing, suggest resilience. The NPA rise is tied to specific borrowers, and legal actions are underway. However, concentration risks and margin pressures pose challenges. If unaddressed, further NPA slippages could push the stock below ₹155.

विज्ञापन

7. Investment Considerations

  • Monitor NPA resolution, especially Gensol insolvency.
  • Track Q2 FY26 margins and cost-control measures.
  • Evaluate QIP capital deployment effectiveness.
  • Assess renewable energy lending trends and bond issuances.

Long-term investors may see the dip as a buying opportunity, given IREDA’s alignment with India’s 500 GW renewable energy goal by 2030. Short-term traders should be cautious due to volatility. Consult a financial advisor before investing.

8. Conclusion

IREDAs Q1 FY26 results highlight challenges in profitability and asset quality, driving a 6% share price drop to ₹159. However, strong revenue and loan book growth, coupled with government support, suggest this is a temporary setback. Close monitoring of NPA resolution and future earnings is crucial, but IREDA’s long-term prospects in the renewable energy sector remain promising.
